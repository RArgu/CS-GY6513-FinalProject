We want to detect insider trading on prediction markets like Polymarket. The idea is to look at resolved contracts —
  where we already know the outcome — and work backwards to find wallets that consistently placed large, correct bets   
  before the news broke publicly. We'd join prediction market transaction data against a news timeline from something
  like GDELT or NewsAPI, and score each wallet on how often they were early and right. Wallets that are statistically
  too prescient get flagged. The big data angle is the multi-source join — blockchain trade logs, news event streams,
  and contract metadata — plus we'd use Spark for streaming and SQL, HDFS/MapReduce for historical backfill, Hive/Tez
  for warehousing, MongoDB for the unstructured news data, and MLlib and WEKA for classification and clustering. The
  long-term goal is to validate the model forward — train on resolved contracts, then apply it to open markets and see
  if the flagged wallets keep being right.
